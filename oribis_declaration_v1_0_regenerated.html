Frozen Canon. A non-verbal relational framework for validating structural coherence through orbs, relations, and motion.
Version: 1.0

Statement

This page publicly declares Codex Oribis v1.0 as a frozen canonical system. Oribis represents meaning through circular primitives (orbs), their spatial relations, and motion states. Oribis does not interpret intent, assign moral value, or decide outcomes. It validates structure only.

What Oribis Is

  • A deterministic framework for testing whether a claim, rule, or system can remain coherent under fixed constraints
  • A visual encoding method based on relation, geometry, and motion rather than words
  • A stability validator that yields: STABLE, TENSION, or COLLAPSE

What Oribis Is Not

  • A replacement for law, courts, governance, or sovereign authority
  • A decision-maker, judge, or enforcement mechanism
  • A predictive model of behavior or outcomes
  • A natural-language translator of authoritative meaning

Frozen in v1.0

  • The Twelve Prime Orbs
  • Canonical invariants and axioms
  • Spatial syntax and motion grammar
  • Stability criteria and falsification rules
  • Error taxonomy (Type I–VII)

Permitted in v1.x

  • Documentation clarity and formatting improvements
  • Examples and demonstration plates
  • Tooling implementations and visual modes
  • Domain applications that do not modify canon

Version Policy

The v1.x series is clarification-only and may not change semantics or behavior. Major versions (v2.0+) may extend Oribis only by additive composition that preserves all v1.0 invariants.

Stability constitutes truth.
Stillness constitutes assertion.
Silence constitutes validity.

Appropriate Uses

  • Structural review of governance models and policy logic
  • Pre-negotiation alignment and contradiction discovery
  • Visualization of constraint-consistency in complex systems
  • Audit-friendly configuration hashing for reproducible analysis

Contact and Attribution

Canonical ownership and publication details may be appended for institutional contexts. Any implementation claiming Oribis compliance should reference Codex Oribis v1.0 and declare its constraint set as DEFAULT_V1 or an explicitly layered extension.

By design, Oribis makes no claim about what should be done. It answers a narrower question: whether a proposed structure can exist without internal contradiction under stated constraints.
